EEG methods reporting · open science · est. 2021

Report EEG methods clearly, consistently, and FAIR.

ARTEM-IS — the Agreed Reporting Template for EEG Methodology, International Standard — is an INCF community initiative. It turns EEG methods reporting into structured, human- and machine-readable summaries, so studies are easier to understand, reproduce, and reuse.

What ARTEM-IS is

Systematic reviews of the EEG literature keep finding the same gap: methods sections rarely state the individual analysis decisions needed to reproduce a study. ARTEM-IS closes that gap.

ARTEM-IS provides community-developed reporting templates delivered through a web application. Researchers fill in a structured form covering study description, experimental design, hardware, data acquisition, pre-processing, measurement, and visualisation — and export a detailed methods summary that both people and machines can read.

The templates are built from evidence: insights from systematic reviews of reporting practice, combined with guidelines for good checklist design. The first released tool, ARTEM-IS for ERP, documents the core methodological decisions of an event-related potential experiment.

The project is hosted by the International Neuroinformatics Coordinating Facility (INCF) and developed in the open, with an active working group and links to the COST Action EEG101 — Fundamentals of Open & Rigorous EEG Science.

Working groups

Within the EEG101 COST Action (WG1 — Reporting Standards), five teams take ARTEM-IS forward.

1

Usability analysis

Studying how researchers use the tool and where the template can be clearer.

2

Training & dissemination

Workshops, tutorials, and materials so labs can teach and adopt ARTEM-IS.

3

Lexicon

A shared terminology so EEG methods are described consistently across studies.

4

Human–AI alignment

Making templated, machine-readable reporting work with automated and AI tooling.

5

Journal adoption

Working with editors to integrate structured reporting into journal workflows.
